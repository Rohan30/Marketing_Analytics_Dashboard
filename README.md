## üîç Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end marketing analytics dashboard built in Tableau, designed to monitor customer acquisition, engagement, conversion, and revenue performance across multiple digital marketing channels.

The dashboard enables stakeholders to:

- Track top-of-funnel to bottom-of-funnel performance
- Compare channel effectiveness
- Evaluate marketing revenue, CLV, and CAC

The analysis is demonstrated using October 2023 data, with month-over-month (MoM) comparisons to highlight performance changes.

## üìå Dashboard Structure

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Overview ‚Äì Marketing Performance Snapshot

This screen provides a high-level executive summary of marketing health.

**Key Metrics Included:**

- Total Visitors & Unique Visitors (with MoM change)
- Average Session Duration
- Bounce Rate
- Lead Generation Rate
- Click-Through Rate (CTR)
- Lead Conversion Rate
- Customer Retention Rate
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) by channel
- Device Split (Phones, Tablets, PCs)
- Engagement Metrics (Likes, Comments, Shares)

**Funnel View:**

- TOFU: Bounce Rate, CTR
- MOFU: Lead Generation Rate
- BOFU: Lead Conversion Rate, Retention Rate

This view helps identify where users drop off and whether performance issues stem from traffic quality, engagement, or conversion inefficiencies.

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ Channel Analysis ‚Äì Organic, Referral & Social Media

The second screen deep-dives into channel-level performance, enabling side-by-side comparison.

For each channel, the dashboard shows:

- Total vs Unique Visitors
- Share of total traffic
- Month-over-month growth
- Marketing Revenue contribution
- Sub-channel mix  
  - Organic: SEO, Content Marketing, Others  
  - Referral: Affiliate, Blogs, Email Campaigns  
  - Social Media: Instagram, Facebook, YouTube  
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) by sub-channel

This allows stakeholders to identify:

- High-volume but low-quality channels
- High-CLV sub-channels worth scaling
- Channels driving growth vs volatility

---

## üìà Key Insights Highlighted

- Social Media shows strong MoM growth in visitors and revenue, indicating scaling potential
- Organic traffic delivers stable, high-quality users with strong CLV
- Referral channels demonstrate mixed performance, requiring sub-channel optimization
- Higher engagement does not always translate to higher conversions, emphasizing the need for funnel-level analysis
- CLV variation across sub-channels supports reallocation of marketing spend
